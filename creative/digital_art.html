Digital Art

Spring 2018 | Stanford, CA

Connections

Connections is my attempt to map out and reflect upon the various relationships that have influenced me throughout my life in both big and small ways. To create this piece I first spent several hours planning out which individuals I wanted to include, assigning each person a unique ID, and sorting them into various categories, such as my mom's extended family, Legacy, or my friends from the BOSP Madrid program. Next I collected all of the raw imagery for the network, which involved searching through Facebook, Instagram, and my own personal photo archive. The final step of the project was simply to place all of my nodes and edges on a single canvas. This proved to be much more time-consuming than I imagined; this step alone took about 12-13 hours and led to my first and only all-nighter at Stanford.

160

For our self-portrait assignment I created 160, which is a visual reflection of a majority of the academic work that I completed throughout my four years as an undergrad. The piece's title comes from the exact number of units I had taken leading up to my senior spring. As a digital hoarder who meticulously archives everything in the cloud, I had easy access to all of the coursework I had ever completed, including CS scripts, technical risk assessments, Spanish reading reflections, research papers, and mathematical problem sets. I grabbed random snippets from as many of these assignments as possible in order to create the giant blob of text that serves as portrait's body. The outline of my head comes from a shot captured by my friend Alex during the summer we spent working in New York City.

Freestyle Lab

Freestyle Lab is an interactive work built using the p5.js library that gives users a glimpse into my identity as a dancer. Specifically, users can pick between several songs to which I've choreographed in the past and are subsequently presented with an organic, dynamic visualization that tries to capture some of the challenges of choreographing and freesytling. Note: I recommend viewing on an actual computer using a modern browser like Chrome or Safari.
