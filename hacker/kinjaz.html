Kinjaz Fan Site

August 2015 | San Jose, CA

Combining my love of CS with my love of dance

While working as a TA for the Computer Simulations and Interactive Media course for Stanford Pre-Collegiates Summer Institutes this past summer, I spent a lot of time helping kids with HTML and CSS. Most of this time was spent on the basics of these two languages, which made me feel pretty comfortable since I did not have much of a web development background. After the course ended and I returned home, I decided that I wanted to play around with these technologies even more, which ultimately led to the decision to try and build a small website.

A glimpse into the mission statement of the Kinjaz

Around the time, one of my favorite urban dance crews, the Kinjaz, had just launched a website of their own to keep fans updated with announcements, dance videos, and merchandise. As a result, I decided I would a Kinjaz website of my own by modeling some aspects of their site while adding some new features of my own. Over the next couple of days, I slowly built up my site, starting with a few simple pages and then adding images, tables, a navigation bar, and even dynamically generated footers. Google was definitely my friend as it connected me to a lot of helpful resources as I played around with HTML and CSS. I found the tutorials from W3Schools to be especially useful because of their simple instructions and wide topic coverage.

I love how the Kinjaz infuse the ninja them into their urban choreography

Overall I enjoyed the experience of learning the basics of web development at my own pace. Rather than have to learn about specific topics and conventions as I would in a class, I was able to focus on the areas that I was actually interested in. I had never taught myself a programming skill before, and my experience building the Kinjaz site taught me to be self-reliant when it came to figuring out bugs in my code. Thus, I would definitely say that working on my own made me a more confident programmer in general. Moving forward, my next goal is to try and build my own personal website from scratch in order to host my digital portfolio.
